Study Overview

This analysis examines health disparities between sexual and gender minority (SGM) and majority youth across bullying, substance use, and mental health domains using nationally representative weighted survey data.

Methods

Data Source

  • 2023 Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance System (YRBSS), CDC
  • 20,103 usable questionnaires from U.S. adolescents in grades 9–12
  • Complex survey design with stratification, clustering, and weighting

SGM Classification

  • Sexual identity (q64): Gay/lesbian, bisexual, other, questioning → minority
  • Sexual contact (q63 × q2): Same-sex or both-sex contacts → minority
  • Gender identity (q65): Transgender or questioning → minority
  • Combined flag: any minority indicator above triggers SGM classification

Mental Health Composite

  • 7 binary indicators: sadness, ideation, planning, attempt, treatment, poor MH frequency, cognitive difficulty
  • PCA-derived weights with clinical severity adjustments
  • Normalized 0–10 scale, stratified at clinical cutoffs: Low (≤1), Moderate (1–6), High (>6)

Modeling Pipeline

  • Multiple imputation via MICE (8 imputations) with domain-aware pruning
  • LightGBM with survey weights, class weights, and GridSearchCV hyperparameter tuning
  • SHAP values for feature importance and subgroup-specific risk factor profiles
  • Post-imputation distributional validation by race and SGM status

All prevalence estimates on this page are weighted using CDC-provided survey weights to produce nationally representative estimates. Full analysis code is available on GitHub.
